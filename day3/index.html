Day 3 — Form to AI

Each group builds one end-to-end data product: a Google Form (application / enrollment) with an image-document upload, stored in a Sheet, then analyzed in Colab and run through your own AI model.

▶ Open Capstone Notebook 📄 Capstone Guide (PDF) 📁 Day 3 Materials (Drive)
Google Form + image upload→ Google Sheet→ Colab: analyze + visualize→ Teachable Machine: identify + confidence

The 5 steps

1

Build the form

A short application / enrollment form with a few fields and one file-upload question for an image document. Link a response Sheet.

2

Collect responses

Fill it a few times (each with an image upload). Answers and the image's Drive link land in the Sheet.

3

Connect & analyze

In the Colab notebook, connect to your Sheet, describe the data, and visualize the responses.

4

Interpret

Write 2–3 sentences: what do the charts say about your applicants/enrollees?

5

Apply the AI

Load your Teachable Machine model; it identifies each uploaded image and reports the confidence level. Flag low-confidence ones for review.

3-hour timetable

0:00–0:15Brief & groups — pick your form type (application or enrollment) and classes for the model.
0:15–1:00Build & collect (45) — make the Google Form with an image upload; gather a few responses.
1:00–1:45Analyze & visualize (45) — connect the notebook to the Sheet; charts + interpretation.
1:45–2:30AI (45) — reuse/train a Teachable Machine model; classify the uploaded images; test confidence.
2:30–3:00Polish & dry-run (30) — findings + 1 recommendation; rehearse the 3-minute story.
Hour 4Presentations & closing.
Form tip: a file-upload question requires responders to sign in with Google, and the uploads save to the form owner's Drive. Run the notebook with that same owner account so it can read the images.

What "done" looks like

  • A working Google Form with an image-document upload + a linked Sheet of responses
  • The notebook connected to the Sheet, with charts + a written interpretation
  • Each uploaded image identified by your model, with its confidence level
  • A confidence read-out (average, and which uploads are below 80% / need review)
  • A 3-minute story: what the form collected → what the data shows → how well the AI identified the documents
