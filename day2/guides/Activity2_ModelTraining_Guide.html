🚶 Fieldwork FIRST — build a REAL image dataset with your own hands

The real work: this is the heaviest collection task and the most real. You cannot train an honest image model on images you didn't gather. You go get REAL documents, from REAL people, and photograph or scan them yourself. Kayo mismo ang kukuha ng totoong dokumento at litrato.

Build your own image set of at least 40 signed + 40 unsigned real document images (80 total minimum). Get real people to sign and to leave blank; photograph or scan forms, papers, slips. Vary the lighting, angle, and paper so the model learns real variation. Pairs/small groups may collect together, but each person submits their own trained result.

PROOF (both required):

  • The RAW image FOLDER (your 80+ collected images), organized into signed / unsigned.
  • PHOTOS + a short VIDEO (20–40 sec) of you actually collecting — people signing, you photographing the documents. This proves the dataset is real and yours.

Then follow the Teachable Machine + Colab steps in the guide below: train on YOUR images, run the classifier over YOUR folder, save the results CSV, and do the confidence EDA. Because the images are real and varied, the confidence scores and the "needs review" cases will be real.

Interpretation: how many did the model get confidently right vs send to review? Where did it struggle (bad lighting? faint signatures?) — that struggle is real because your data is real.

Shared rules: photo AND video both required (no proof = not accepted) · pairs/groups may collect together but each submits their own trained result · reduced/desk data only with teacher's prior permission (message first) · your raw image folder is a required deliverable.
